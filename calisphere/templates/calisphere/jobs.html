Applications Programmer (7299)

The California Digital Library (CDL) is a collaborative effort of the ten campuses of the University of California, designed to enable UC's libraries to continue to meet the informational needs of UC's faculty and students in the 21st century. The CDL assumes a leadership role in providing digital content to the citizens of the state, develops systems that encourage resource sharing, and provides for a focus for the development of strategies and initiatives that guide the University's libraries through the transition to the digital future.

This grant-funded position plays a primary role in conducting "harvests" of digital collections, which involves using various protocols to import metadata from various library, archive, and museum systems into a centralized database that we maintain here at the CDL.

Working under the direction of the metadata harvest Technical Lead, and working closely with the Operations Manager, incumbent will be trained on performing steps necessary to set up and harvest new collections. Responsible for performing harvests, assisting with quality assurance, running reports, and modifying and maintaining programs that implement metadata mapping.

Duties

  • Analyzes Open Archive Initiative (OAI) and other data feeds from contributors.
  • Plans, designs, develops, modifies, debugs and evaluates moderately complex software for metadata harvesting.
  • Analyzes existing software or works to formulate logic for moderately complex new systems and devises moderately complex algorithms.
  • Utilizing a unix command shell and a web browser, initiates and monitors a multi-step data processing pipeline.
  • Follows operations manual and consults with harvester technical lead.
  • Publishes candidate and production solr indexes.
  • Runs reports to assist in the QA process for new indexes.
  • Working with QA analysts, troubleshoots and debugs issues related to filed mapping, new collections, and harvester operations.
  • Gathers and analyzes data to prepare system and programming documentation.
  • Assists with implementation of changes in development, maintenance and system standards and in the creation of software specifications.
  • Regularly reports progress on projects.
  • Attends meetings and participates in phone calls with operations team and data contributors.
  • Uses email, chat, and helpdesk software effectively and professionally.

Skills and Experience

  • Bachelor's degree in computer science, information management or science, a related field, or equivalent combination of training and experience programming data export, transform, and load routines.
  • At least 1 year experience writing accurate data transformation scripts or performing comparable analysis and scripting tasks.
  • 3 years experience working in a unix shell or comparable command line environment.
  • Demonstrated knowledge of data formats such as XML, JSON, or RDF.
  • Experience with common programming tools and editors.
  • Demonstrated ability to follow written documentation and operations manuals.
  • Demonstrated effective communication and interpersonal skills.
  • Self-motivated and works independently and as part of a team. Able to learn effectively and meet deadlines.
  • Ability to learn python and other languages as needed.
  • Demonstrated ability to debug and analyze complex data flows.
  • Familiar with library standards such as OAI-PMH (preferred).
  • Intermediate skills associated with the design, development, modification and deployment of software, including object-oriented programming concepts (preferred).
  • Familiarity with Amazon Web Services and its command line interface (preferred).
  • Knowledge of CouchDB and creation of CouchDB map/reduce jobs (preferred).
  • Knowledge of solr or other lucene based search technology (preferred).

Appointment and salary

This is a 15-month contract appointment with the possibility of extension. This contract position comes with the full array of UC benefits as well as participation of UCRP retirement program. The posting salary is $47,700-$72,900 (Min Range to Mid Range).

This position maps to Payroll Title APPLICATIONS PROGR 2 (Job Code: 7299).
